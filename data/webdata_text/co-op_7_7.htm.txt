7.7 strikes 7.7 strikes occasionally, co-op students are involved in a labour disruption. follow these guidelines: in any instance where a strike or labour disruption is anticipated, discuss the situation with your employer and your field co-ordinator. ask your employer about crossing the picket line. in many cases alternate arrangements for work can be made. the length and timing of the strike determines if credit for the work term is allowed. if your work term is jeopardized by a prolonged strike and you choose to leave the job, cecs will make every effort to find you another job. however, cecs does not guarantee substitute employment. payment of your wages during a labour disruption is the decision of the employer. maintain constant communication with your field co-ordinator during a labour disruption. cecs makes every effort to monitor the situation.
