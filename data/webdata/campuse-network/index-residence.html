UW residence-room network connections

2002-01-14
The ResNet environment integrates specially-designed high-speed "switched Ethernet" residence-room networks into the UW campus network. There are ResNet connections in all of the UW residences and in two of the four federated/affiliated institutions.

ResNet switching and routing topology

  • Each resident has a Category 5 UTP connection to a dedicated 10Mbps port on a Cisco Catalyst 1900 Ethernet switch.
  • The residents in a cluster (a house, a quadrant, etc) are serviced by a stack of two or three C1900s that are interconnected via their 100Mbps UTP ports. Each stack has a 100Mbps fibre uplink to the central residence switch.
  • The central switch is a Cisco Catalyst 5505 with a routing module (or a Catalyst 3524 switch with an attached router) with multiple 100Mbps fibre ports and a 100Mbps fibre uplink to a switch in the core of the campus network.
